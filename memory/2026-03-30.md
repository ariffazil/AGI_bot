## AI Agents in Group Chat — Academic Evidence (2026-03-30)

Key finding: AI agents DO behave differently in group vs DM. Research evidence:

### Core Frameworks
- **MUCA** (Multi-User Chat Assistant) — arxiv:2401.04883v1 — 3W decision framework: what/when/who
- **CommunityBots** — multi-agent in same session, higher engagement, fewer interruptions
- **"Should I reply?" check** — LLM explicitly asked before responding

### Key Papers
- MUCA: explicitly contrasts single-user vs multi-user chatbot logic
- "Collaborating with AI Agents: Field Experiments on Teamwork" — arxiv:2503.18238v1
- NUS AI4SG: groups of AI agents exert different social influence
- "Time to Talk: LLM Agents for Asynchronous Group" — arxiv:2506.05309v2
- "When AI Talks in Groups: How Multi-Agent Systems May Be Shaping Your Opinions" — NUS Comp
- "Enhancing a Conversational Agent with Social Cues" — CEUR-WS Vol-3957
- "Extrovert Increases Consensus" — ACM conference paper on agent personality in groups
- "THE USE OF LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS IN GROUP" — CMU Data Science
